Cave Types

Minecraft caves come in many forms, including lush caves filled with greenery and glow berries, dripstone caves with stalactites and stalagmites, and deep slate layers that feel more dangerous and isolated. Each cave type has unique blocks and threats.

Cave Type Description Unique Features Common Resources Image
Lush Caves A vibrant underground biome full of greenery and natural light. Glow berries, azalea roots, spore blossoms, axolotls Iron, Copper, Clay
Dripstone Caves Jagged cave systems filled with pointed dripstone formations. Stalactites and stalagmites, dripstone blocks Copper, Iron
Deep Dark A dark, eerie biome deep underground, home to the Warden. Sculk sensors, ancient cities, Warden Redstone, Diamonds

Cave Generation

Cave Type Description Unique Features Image
Cheese Caves Large open areas with wide caverns and scattered ores. High ceilings, vast rooms, lava lakes
Spaghetti Caves Thin, twisting tunnels resembling spaghetti strands. Long narrow paths, difficult navigation
Noodle Caves Thin but longer caves than spaghetti, often interwoven. Crisscrossing paths, tighter spaces
Aquifer Caves Water-filled underground caves that generate below Y=0. Underwater exploration, waterlogging risks

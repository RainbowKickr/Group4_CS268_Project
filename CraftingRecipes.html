Crafting Shape

Crafting in Minecraft is pretty simple once you get to know it but there are some tricks to it when it comes to crafting shapes. There's three categories of crafting shapes, shapeless, shaped, and fixed. Shapeless crafting recipes are simple, you're ingredients can go into any slot on the crafting grid and you'll be able to craft your item. Shapeless is a little more restrictive, your crafting ingredients have to form a specific shape, for example, a 2x2 square, 1x3 rectangle, and x-shaped are all examples of crafting shapes, these crafting shapes can be placed anywhere they can fit in a 2x2 or 3x3 crafting grid. Fixed crafting shape is similar to shaped in that you create a shape except that it only works in one position, this is usually reserved for bigger crafting recipes that take up most of the 3x3 crafting grid.

Types of Blocks

There are certain categories of blocks that will be helpful to know when crafting, some examples are wood and stone. There are eleven types of wood and they can all be used to craft the same items with the only difference between any of them being color, except that crimson and warped wood are fire resistant. Stone is a much bigger category with multiple subcategories within it, natural, polished/smooth, and bricks. These include the materials stone, cobblestone, mossy cobblestone, smooth stone, stone bricks, mossy stone bricks, granite, polished granite, diorite, polished diorite, andesite, polished andesite, deepslate, cobbled deepslate, polished deepslate, deepslate brick, deepslate tiles, tuff, polished tuff, tuff bricks, bricks, mud bricks, resin bricks, sandstone, smooth sandstone, cut sandstone, red sandstone, smooth red sandstone, cut red sandstone, prismarine, prismarine bricks, nether brick, red nether brick, blackstone, polished blackstone, polished black stone bricks, end stone, and purpur.
